The Salesforce case note that became training data. The reentry program that became a surveillance pipeline. The AI that learned to predict the people it was built to control. ProPublica found COMPAS in 2016. Here is what came after.

In 2016 ProPublica published "Machine Bias." They found that COMPAS — an AI risk assessment tool used in criminal sentencing across America — flagged Black defendants as high risk at twice the rate of white defendants for the same offense profile. The tool's creator said the algorithm was fair. The data said otherwise.

That was the warning.

Here is what was built after the warning.

What COMPAS Was

COMPAS (Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions) was a questionnaire and scoring algorithm used by judges to predict recidivism — the likelihood that a defendant would reoffend. Judges used the score in sentencing decisions. Parole boards used it in release decisions.

The training data was historical criminal justice records. The historical records reflected decades of biased policing, biased prosecution, and biased sentencing. The algorithm learned the bias as ground truth. It then predicted that bias forward — into the futures of people who had not yet done anything.

COMPAS used sentencing data. Court records. Arrest histories.

What came after COMPAS uses something much richer.

The Free Seat Program

Salesforce operates a nonprofit licensing program called the Power of Us. Organizations classified as nonprofits receive ten free Salesforce seats. Additional seats at deeply discounted rates. The program is presented as philanthropy — technology for social good, empowering organizations doing mission-driven work.

Reentry programs across America run on these seats. Workforce development organizations. Formerly-incarcerated employment programs. Court-referred job training pipelines. The organizations that receive people directly from probation departments and place them in jobs as a condition of their supervised release.

Every participant who enters one of these programs becomes a Salesforce record.

Object: Contact (Participant) ├── Court referral date ├── Probation officer (lookup) ├── Criminal history summary ├── EBT/SNAP enrollment status ├── Training completion percentage ├── Days employed ├── Employer (lookup → Account) ├── Case status: ACTIVE └── Case notes: [unstructured text field]

The participant did not choose to enter this Salesforce instance. Their probation condition made program enrollment mandatory. Mandatory enrollment made data entry mandatory. The data entry feeds Salesforce's infrastructure.

The charity is the data collection.
The free seat is the pipeline.

The Case Note

Structured fields tell the model what. Case notes tell the model how. Case workers, program directors, employer partners, and probation officers write unstructured text notes in Salesforce about participants continuously. These notes are the richest data in the system.

Here is a case note. You have seen this note. You have read this note. Maybe you were this note.

DATE: [week 3 of placement]
CASE WORKER: [program staff]
NOTE:

Participant showed up late twice this week.
Seems disengaged. Employer expressed concerns
about attitude. Recommend monitoring.

What the model learns from this note as labeled training data:

FEATURES extracted: "showed up late twice" → negative behavioral signal "seems disengaged" → negative engagement signal "employer concerns" → negative placement signal "attitude" → negative compliance signal LABEL assigned: RECOMMEND MONITORING → escalate surveillance MODEL learns: This surface pattern → increase supervision Increased supervision → more case notes generated More case notes → more training data confirming pattern Pattern confirmed → model predicts it in next participant

The word "seems" is in the note. The case worker was uncertain. The observation was subjective. The hedge is right there in the text.

The model does not process hedges. The model processes tokens. "Seems disengaged" trains identically to "is disengaged." The uncertainty that existed when the note was written disappears when it becomes a training signal. The subjectivity becomes ground truth.

What "Disengaged" Actually Means

I worked one of these programs. I picked up trash on the freeway. I worked beside a man I will call Chad.

Chad's wife was killed by a drunk driver on his second day on the job. That day — day two of the only job the record would let him have, on the most dangerous road-adjacent work available, next to the traffic that never stops.

He came back the next day. And the day after. For three years. I watched him do it.

Three years later the program threw him a work anniversary. They called it Chad's Work Anniversary. They gave a speech. He cried.

Somewhere in a Salesforce instance, Chad has case notes. Some of those notes probably say "seems disengaged." Because grief, when observed by someone who does not know your history, looks exactly like disengagement.

The model does not know his wife died on day two. That field does not exist.

The model learned: this pattern = risk.

The next participant who is quiet — who carries something privately, who sees the system clearly and will not perform enthusiasm for it, whose stillness reads as attitude to someone who needs to fill a field — gets flagged before they have done anything. The prediction shapes the intervention. The intervention shapes the outcome. The outcome confirms the prediction.

The cage learned to build itself.

The Pipeline COMPAS Never Had

COMPAS had criminal justice records. One dimension. One system.

The Salesforce reentry pipeline has:

DATA FLOWING INTO SALESFORCE NONPROFIT INSTANCES: Criminal history → from court referral Probation compliance → check-in records, violations, PO notes Employment behavior → attendance, retention, employer feedback Financial data → EBT enrollment, benefit coordination, wage records Training history → completions, certifications, attendance Housing status → address, stability metrics Case notes → years of unstructured text observations Longitudinal outcomes → what happened over years of supervision SCALE: Tens of thousands of participants Across hundreds of organizations In dozens of cities Over more than a decade All flowing through Salesforce infrastructure

In 2024 Salesforce launched Agentforce — autonomous AI agents that operate inside Salesforce instances using the data in those instances. Einstein AI. Data Cloud. Predictive analytics built into the CRM.

The model that runs on this data predicts behavior for this population better than any external system can. Because no external system has this data. Only Salesforce does.

That model gets sold back to the systems that generated the data: courts, probation departments, government grant programs, employers running background-check-integrated hiring pipelines.

The participants' data trained the model. The model now makes decisions about people like them. They were not asked. They had no choice. Their probation condition put them in the room. The room had a Salesforce instance.

The Financial Architecture

Understand what actually flows through the program before you believe it is what it says it is.

WHAT THE PARTICIPANT THINKS: Wages: $100/day × 3 days = $300/week Benefits: EBT/SNAP enrollment (food assistance) Training: Free job skills (2 days/week) Total: $300 cash + food support WHAT IS ACTUALLY HAPPENING: Your labor (3 days dangerous work) → program counts as "employment outcome" for grant reporting Your EBT enrollment → benefit stream drawn against to pay for mandatory training Your training attendance (2 unpaid days) → charged to your EBT without disclosure → program reports you as "certified" for next grant cycle Your body in the program → generates grant draw from state → generates Salesforce data record for AI training PROGRAM RECEIVES: Grant funding per participant enrolled EBT offset for training costs (your food money) Labor value of your work AI training data from your case record YOU RECEIVE: $300/week (funded by the same grant that funds the program) EBT balance minus training offset (you paid for the mandatory class) One compliant week (you were not violated) A Salesforce record labeled with your behavior

The wages come from the grant. The grant comes from your enrollment. You are paid with money the program received because of you, while your food benefits pay for the training you are required to attend as a condition of the supervision that mandated your enrollment.

Every node in this pipeline — the program, the employer, the background check company, the state grant committee — has a financial incentive for your continued participation. Completion means the funding stream ends. Extended supervision means the funding stream continues. There is no financial incentive in this pipeline to actually finish your case.

It was never a sentence that ended.
It was a sentence that changed its name.
